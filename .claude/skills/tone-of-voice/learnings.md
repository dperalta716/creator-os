## 2026-05-12 — Major skill rework from YouTube scripts session

1. Generic rant questions don't work for enthusiastic practitioners. Journey-based questions (where were you → what changed → where are you now) draw out natural voice AND unique perspective simultaneously.
2. Don't ask format-specific questions ("how would you do a cold open?"). Users may not know format conventions. Instead, use pre-built format templates and let users react to their content IN that format.
3. Calibration must stay on ONE topic until approved, then test generalization on a second topic. Jumping between topics makes iteration impossible.
4. Proactive follow-ups ("does this sound like you?", "what would you say differently?") help users who can't articulate what's off.
5. Verbal tics should be surfaced as a decision, not silently excluded. Some are intentional style.
6. Sub-agent validation (guide-only, no conversation context) catches gaps in the guide's specificity.
7. Reference examples in the guide give any AI concrete voice targets to match — rules alone aren't enough.
8. Every question should end with an open invitation to keep going ("feel free to just keep going").
9. The user's raw dictation/rewrites during calibration are the best voice signal. When they say "here's how I'd actually say it," that IS the voice. Work from their version, not yours.
10. The journey extraction questions should be separated: content space/audience is one question, what you've figured out is a separate question. Don't bundle them — let people go deep on each.
11. "Where were you before" should also ask "what do you see other people struggling with" — captures both personal and audience pain points in one natural flow.
